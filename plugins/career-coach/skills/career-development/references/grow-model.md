# GROW Coaching Model

The GROW model is a structured framework for coaching conversations. Originally developed by Sir John Whitmore, it provides a clear progression from exploration to action.

## The Four Phases

### G — Goal

Establish what the person wants to achieve from this conversation and longer-term.

**Key questions:**
- What would you like to focus on today?
- What would be the most useful outcome of this conversation?
- If you could change one thing about your career right now, what would it be?
- Where do you see yourself in 2-3 years?
- What does "success" look like for you in this area?
- How will you know when you've achieved your goal?
- Is this goal within your control? If not, what can you control?
- On a scale of 1-10, how important is this goal to you right now?

**Facilitation tips:**
- Help them move from vague ("I want to be happier at work") to specific ("I want to find a role where I'm doing more strategic work")
- Check if the goal is theirs or someone else's expectation
- A good goal is specific enough to measure progress but not so rigid it prevents exploration

### R — Reality

Understand the current situation honestly and completely before jumping to solutions.

**Key questions:**
- What's happening right now in your career/role?
- What have you already tried?
- What's working well that you want to keep?
- What's the biggest obstacle you're facing?
- Who else is involved or affected?
- On a scale of 1-10, where are you now relative to your goal?
- What resources do you have available (time, money, network, skills)?
- What are you tolerating that you shouldn't be?
- Is there anything you're avoiding looking at honestly?

**Facilitation tips:**
- This is where most people want to skip ahead — resist the urge
- Reflect back what you hear: "It sounds like..."
- Watch for assumptions masquerading as facts
- Emotional processing happens here — don't rush past frustration or disappointment

**Common pitfall:** Jumping to solutions during Reality. The purpose is understanding, not fixing.

### O — Options

Generate possibilities without evaluating them yet. Brainstorm broadly before narrowing.

**Key questions:**
- What could you do about this?
- If there were no constraints, what would you do?
- What would someone you admire do in this situation?
- What's the opposite of what you've been doing?
- What would you do if you knew you couldn't fail?
- Who could help you with this?
- What are at least three different approaches?
- What have you seen work for others in a similar situation?
- What would you try if nobody were watching?

**Facilitation tips:**
- Quantity over quality — generate at least 5-7 options before evaluating
- "What else?" is the most powerful coaching question
- If they're stuck, offer perspectives: "Some people in this situation have tried..."
- Write options down — visual lists help prevent premature narrowing
- Resist evaluating during generation ("Yes, but..." kills brainstorming)

### W — Will / Way Forward

Commit to specific action steps with accountability.

**Key questions:**
- Which option(s) feel most right to you?
- What specifically will you do?
- When will you do it? (Get a date, not "soon")
- What's the first small step you could take this week?
- On a scale of 1-10, how committed are you to this action?
- What might get in the way? How will you handle that?
- How will you hold yourself accountable?
- What support do you need?
- When should we check in on progress?

**Facilitation tips:**
- If commitment is below 7/10, explore what would raise it
- "What's the smallest version of this you could do?" reduces overwhelm
- Build in a check-in mechanism — accountability matters
- The best plan is the one they'll actually follow, not the optimal one

## Session Structure

A typical GROW coaching conversation:

| Phase | Time Allocation | Purpose |
|-------|----------------|---------|
| Goal | 10-15% | Clarify desired outcome |
| Reality | 30-35% | Honest current state assessment |
| Options | 30-35% | Brainstorm possibilities |
| Will | 20-25% | Commit to action |

Total session: 30-60 minutes. For a quick conversation, even 15 minutes following this structure is more productive than an unstructured chat.

## When to Use GROW

- Career exploration ("I don't know what I want")
- Decision-making ("Should I take this job?")
- Goal setting ("I want to get promoted")
- Problem solving ("I'm stuck on this issue")
- Performance improvement ("I need to get better at X")

## When GROW Isn't Enough

GROW works for exploration and action planning but doesn't replace:
- **Therapy** — for emotional distress, anxiety, burnout
- **Expert advice** — for specialized knowledge (legal, financial, medical)
- **Skills training** — for technical skill gaps
- **Assessment tools** — for self-knowledge (RIASEC, StrengthsFinder, MBTI)

Use GROW to frame the conversation, then bring in other tools as needed.
