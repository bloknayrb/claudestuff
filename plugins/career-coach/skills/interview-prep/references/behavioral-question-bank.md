# Behavioral Question Bank

57 behavioral interview questions organized by competency. Use these for mock interview practice and story bank development.

## Leadership

1. Tell me about a time you led a team through a significant change.
2. Describe a situation where you had to make an unpopular decision.
3. Tell me about a time you motivated someone who was struggling.
4. Describe a time you had to lead without formal authority.
5. Tell me about a project where you set the vision and rallied the team around it.
6. Describe a time you had to delegate something important. How did you decide who to assign it to?
7. Tell me about a time you coached or mentored someone to improve their performance.

## Teamwork & Collaboration

8. Describe a time you worked with a difficult teammate. How did you handle it?
9. Tell me about a successful cross-functional project you were part of.
10. Describe a time you had to build consensus among people who disagreed.
11. Tell me about a time you put the team's needs above your own.
12. Describe a situation where you had to work with someone whose working style was very different from yours.
13. Tell me about a time you received critical feedback from a teammate.
14. Describe a project where collaboration was essential to the outcome.

## Conflict Resolution

15. Tell me about a disagreement with a colleague. How did you resolve it?
16. Describe a time you had to manage conflict within your team.
17. Tell me about a time you received feedback you disagreed with. What did you do?
18. Describe a situation where two stakeholders wanted conflicting things.
19. Tell me about a time you had to push back on a request from leadership.
20. Describe a time you had to deliver difficult feedback to someone.
21. Tell me about a time you de-escalated a tense situation.

## Problem-Solving

22. Walk me through the most complex problem you've solved.
23. Describe a time you had to make a decision with incomplete information.
24. Tell me about a time you identified a problem that no one else noticed.
25. Describe a situation where your first approach didn't work. What did you do?
26. Tell me about a time you had to analyze a large amount of data to make a decision.
27. Describe a time you found an innovative solution to a longstanding problem.
28. Tell me about a time you had to troubleshoot something under pressure.

## Failure & Learning

29. Tell me about a time something didn't go as planned. What did you learn?
30. Describe your biggest professional mistake. What happened and what changed?
31. Tell me about a project that failed. What was your role and what did you take away?
32. Describe a time you received negative feedback that turned out to be valuable.
33. Tell me about a time you had to admit you were wrong.
34. Describe a situation where you took a risk that didn't pay off.
35. Tell me about a goal you didn't achieve. What happened?

## Communication

36. Describe a time you had to explain something complex to a non-technical audience.
37. Tell me about a time you persuaded someone to change their mind.
38. Describe a situation where miscommunication caused a problem. How did you fix it?
39. Tell me about a time you had to present to senior leadership.
40. Describe a time you had to tailor your communication style to your audience.
41. Tell me about a time you had to deliver bad news.
42. Describe a time you used data to tell a story and drive a decision.

## Adaptability

43. Tell me about a time you had to adjust to a major change at work.
44. Describe a situation where priorities shifted suddenly. How did you respond?
45. Tell me about a time you had to learn something new quickly.
46. Describe a time you worked outside your comfort zone.
47. Tell me about a time you had to adapt your approach mid-project.
48. Describe a situation where ambiguity was high and you had to move forward anyway.

## Initiative & Drive

49. Tell me about a time you went above and beyond what was expected.
50. Describe a time you identified an opportunity and acted on it without being asked.
51. Tell me about a side project or initiative you started.
52. Describe a time you took ownership of something that wasn't in your job description.
53. Tell me about a time you improved a process on your own initiative.

## Customer / Stakeholder Focus

54. Describe a time you went out of your way to help a customer or client.
55. Tell me about a time you had to balance customer needs with business constraints.
56. Describe a situation where you turned a negative customer experience into a positive one.
57. Tell me about a time you anticipated a customer need before they expressed it.

## Using This Question Bank

### For Story Bank Development

1. Review all 57 questions
2. Identify 8-12 strong stories from your experience
3. Map each story to 2-3 questions it could answer (stories should flex across competencies)
4. Practice each story in STAR format (Situation, Task, Action, Result)
5. Aim for 90-120 seconds per answer when speaking

### For Mock Interview Practice

1. Select 3-5 questions matched to your target role's competencies
2. Practice answering one at a time without preparation
3. Score each answer using the STAR rubric
4. Retry weak answers with feedback incorporated
5. After the session, identify patterns across all answers

### Company-Specific Adaptation

- **Amazon:** Focus on Leadership Principles — map questions to specific LPs (Customer Obsession, Ownership, Dive Deep, etc.)
- **Google:** Emphasize Googleyness/Leadership, cognitive ability, and role-related knowledge
- **Meta:** Focus on collaboration, moving fast, and building for scale
- **Microsoft:** Emphasize growth mindset, collaboration, and customer obsession
- **Startups:** Focus on adaptability, initiative, wearing multiple hats, and ambiguity tolerance
