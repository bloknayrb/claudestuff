# Networking Scripts & Templates

Networking is the most effective job search channel. These templates provide starting points — always personalize them to sound like yourself.

## LinkedIn Connection Requests

### Warm Outreach (Shared Connection or Background)

```
Hi [Name], I noticed we both worked at [Company] / graduated from [School] /
are members of [Group]. I'm currently [your situation] and would love to connect.
Your work in [their area] is really interesting.
```

### Cold Outreach (No Shared Connection)

```
Hi [Name], I came across your profile while researching [industry/role/company].
Your background in [specific thing] caught my attention because [genuine reason].
I'd love to connect and learn more about your experience.
```

### After Meeting Someone

```
Hi [Name], great meeting you at [event/context]. I really enjoyed our conversation
about [topic]. Would love to stay connected.
```

**Tips:**
- Keep it under 300 characters (LinkedIn limit for connection notes)
- Reference something specific — shows you actually looked at their profile
- Don't ask for anything in the connection request itself

## Informational Interview Requests

### LinkedIn Message (After Connecting)

```
Hi [Name], thanks for connecting! I'm currently [exploring opportunities in X /
considering a transition to Y / researching careers in Z]. I've been really
impressed by [specific thing about their career path].

Would you be open to a 20-minute call so I could learn about your experience
at [Company] / in [role]? I'd love to hear about [specific question].

I'm flexible on timing — happy to work around your schedule. No worries at
all if you're too busy right now.
```

### Email (When You Have Their Email)

**Subject:** Quick question about [their role/company] from a fellow [shared connection]

```
Hi [Name],

[Shared connection] suggested I reach out to you, OR I found your profile
while researching [area] and your background really stood out.

I'm currently [your situation] and I'm exploring [what you're exploring].
Your experience [specific detail] is exactly the kind of path I'm interested in.

Would you have 20 minutes for a quick call or coffee chat? I have a few
specific questions about [topic] and would really value your perspective.

Thanks for considering it — I know your time is valuable.

Best,
[Your Name]
```

## Informational Interview Questions

Prepare 5-7 questions. Here are strong options organized by category:

### About Their Path

- How did you end up in your current role? Was it planned or did it evolve?
- What did you do before [current role]? How did that prepare you?
- What surprised you most about working in [field/company]?

### About the Role/Industry

- What does a typical week look like for you?
- What skills are most important for success in [role]?
- What's the biggest challenge in [industry] right now?
- How has [industry] changed in the last few years?

### About Getting In

- If you were breaking into [field] today, what would you focus on?
- What's the most common mistake you see from people trying to enter [field]?
- Are there any skills or certifications that would make a candidate stand out?

### About the Company

- What's the culture like at [Company]? What type of person thrives there?
- What do you like most about working at [Company]?
- How are teams structured? How much autonomy do individuals have?

### Closing Question

- Is there anyone else you'd recommend I talk to?
- Is there anything I should be reading or following to learn more about [field]?

**Rules:**
- Do NOT ask "Can you get me a job?" or "Are there openings?"
- If they offer to refer you, accept graciously — but don't ask for it
- Stick to 20 minutes unless they extend the conversation
- Take notes — you'll want to reference specific things in your follow-up

## Follow-Up Messages

### After an Informational Interview

**Send within 24 hours:**

```
Hi [Name],

Thank you so much for taking the time to chat today. Your insight about
[specific thing they said] was really helpful — it's shifted how I'm thinking
about [topic].

I'm going to [specific action based on their advice] and I'll keep you
posted on how it goes.

If there's ever anything I can help you with, please don't hesitate to ask.

Best,
[Your Name]
```

### Keeping in Touch (Monthly/Quarterly)

```
Hi [Name],

I wanted to share a quick update — since we last spoke, I [action you took
based on their advice / relevant career update]. Your suggestion about
[specific advice] made a real difference.

I also came across [article/resource/event] that made me think of our
conversation about [topic]. Thought you might find it interesting.

Hope things are going well at [Company]!

Best,
[Your Name]
```

### Asking for a Referral

**Only after building the relationship (not the first conversation):**

```
Hi [Name],

I noticed [Company] has an opening for [Role] that looks like a great fit
for my background, especially given [specific alignment]. Here's the listing:
[link]

Would you be comfortable referring me? I've attached my resume for reference.
I completely understand if you're not able to — no pressure at all.

Thanks either way!
[Your Name]
```

## Networking Event Tips

### Before the Event
- Research speakers and attendees if possible
- Prepare a 30-second intro: who you are, what you're working on, what you're looking for
- Set a goal: "I'll have 3 meaningful conversations" (not "talk to everyone")

### At the Event
- Ask questions, don't pitch yourself
- "What are you working on?" is the best opener
- Listen more than you talk
- Exchange LinkedIn info or business cards
- Take brief notes on your phone after each conversation

### After the Event
- Connect on LinkedIn within 48 hours with a personalized note
- Follow up with anyone you had a particularly good conversation with
- Share relevant resources that came up in your conversations

## Follow-Up Cadence

| Timeframe | Action |
|-----------|--------|
| Same day | Connect on LinkedIn with personalized note |
| Within 24 hours | Send thank-you email (after informational interviews) |
| 1-2 weeks | Share a relevant article or resource |
| 1 month | Quick update on how you applied their advice |
| Quarterly | Stay-in-touch message with value (article, event, introduction) |

The goal is to be helpful and genuine, not transactional. Give before you ask.
