# Resume Action Verbs

Strong resume bullets start with powerful action verbs. The right verb communicates both what you did and the scale of your impact.

## Verbs by Impact Type

### Leadership & Management

Led, Directed, Managed, Oversaw, Supervised, Coordinated, Orchestrated, Spearheaded, Championed, Mentored, Coached, Guided, Delegated, Mobilized, Recruited, Hired, Onboarded, Evaluated, Promoted

### Creation & Innovation

Built, Created, Designed, Developed, Engineered, Architected, Launched, Founded, Established, Introduced, Pioneered, Prototyped, Invented, Authored, Produced, Constructed, Formulated, Devised

### Improvement & Optimization

Improved, Optimized, Streamlined, Revamped, Modernized, Upgraded, Enhanced, Accelerated, Automated, Simplified, Consolidated, Restructured, Transformed, Overhauled, Refined, Standardized

### Analysis & Research

Analyzed, Evaluated, Assessed, Investigated, Researched, Identified, Diagnosed, Audited, Forecasted, Modeled, Quantified, Measured, Benchmarked, Surveyed, Mapped, Discovered

### Communication & Collaboration

Presented, Communicated, Negotiated, Advocated, Influenced, Persuaded, Facilitated, Mediated, Translated, Briefed, Reported, Published, Documented, Articulated, Conveyed

### Delivery & Execution

Delivered, Executed, Implemented, Deployed, Shipped, Completed, Achieved, Accomplished, Fulfilled, Resolved, Processed, Administered, Maintained, Operated, Serviced

### Growth & Revenue

Generated, Increased, Grew, Expanded, Scaled, Captured, Secured, Won, Acquired, Converted, Monetized, Drove, Boosted, Maximized, Multiplied

### Cost Reduction & Efficiency

Reduced, Decreased, Eliminated, Cut, Saved, Prevented, Minimized, Contained, Avoided, Conserved, Lowered, Mitigated

## Weak Verb Blacklist

These verbs weaken your resume. Replace them with stronger alternatives.

| Weak Verb/Phrase | Why It's Weak | Stronger Alternative |
|-----------------|---------------|---------------------|
| Responsible for | Describes a job description, not an action | Led, Managed, Owned |
| Assisted with | Hides your specific contribution | Supported by [specific action] |
| Helped | Vague, no individual ownership | Enabled, Facilitated, Contributed [specific thing] |
| Worked on | Generic, says nothing about what you did | Built, Designed, Developed, Analyzed |
| Participated in | Passive, no clear role | Contributed [specific deliverable] to |
| Handled | Vague catch-all | Managed, Processed, Resolved, Coordinated |
| Was tasked with | Passive voice, removes agency | [Just state what you did] |
| Duties included | Job description language, not resume language | [Start with an action verb] |
| Utilized | Unnecessarily formal for "used" | Used, Leveraged, Applied |
| Collaborated with | Often too vague | Partnered with [team] to deliver [result] |

## The Impact Formula

Every bullet should follow:

**[Strong Action Verb] + [What You Did] + [Measurable Result]**

### Examples

**Weak:** "Responsible for managing the team's quarterly reports"
**Strong:** "Led quarterly reporting for a 12-person team, reducing report generation time by 30% through process automation"

**Weak:** "Helped with customer issues"
**Strong:** "Resolved 150+ customer escalations monthly, improving satisfaction scores from 3.2 to 4.6"

**Weak:** "Worked on the marketing campaign"
**Strong:** "Designed and executed email campaign reaching 50K subscribers, generating $200K in pipeline"

**Weak:** "Participated in the hiring process"
**Strong:** "Screened 200+ candidates and conducted 40 technical interviews, hiring 8 engineers in Q3"

**Weak:** "Was responsible for the database migration"
**Strong:** "Architected and executed migration of 2TB production database to PostgreSQL with zero downtime"

## Tips for Choosing Verbs

1. **Match the verb to the impact level** — "Spearheaded" implies you initiated it; "Contributed to" implies supporting role. Be honest.
2. **Vary your verbs** — Don't start every bullet with "Led" or "Managed." Mix leadership, creation, and delivery verbs.
3. **Use present tense for current role** — "Lead cross-functional team" not "Led cross-functional team" (for your current job only).
4. **Avoid adverbs** — "Successfully led" is weaker than "Led." The result should show the success.
5. **Be specific about scope** — "Managed" could mean 3 people or 300. Include the number.
